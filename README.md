# Netflix-Content-Strategy-Analysis

🎬 Cracking the Code: An Inside Look at Netflix’s Content Strategy

About the Project

Netflix offers thousands of movies and TV shows across different genres, countries, and audience groups. 
In this project, I analyzed Netflix’s content catalog to understand content trends, audience targeting, regional distribution, and content growth over time. 
The goal was to understand Netflix’s content strategy through data and derive meaningful business insights from it.

Along with exploratory data analysis, I also built a simple recommendation system that suggests similar titles based on their genres and descriptions.



Tools & Technologies

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Scikit-learn
* WordCloud

What I Analyzed

* Distribution of Movies and TV Shows
* Most popular genres on Netflix
* Top content-producing countries
* Rating and audience trends
* Content growth over the years
* Most featured directors
* Release year vs. year added analysis
* Content descriptions through word clouds

Recommendation System

To make the project more practical, I built a content-based recommendation system using TF-IDF and Cosine Similarity. It recommends titles that are similar in terms of genre and description.

Key Insights

* Movies make up a larger portion of Netflix’s catalog than TV Shows.
* Drama and International Movies are among the most common genres.
* The United States contributes the highest number of titles.
* Netflix saw significant content growth between 2016 and 2020.
* Most content is targeted toward mature audiences through TV-MA and TV-14 ratings.
